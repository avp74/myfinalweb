What is Phishing?

Phishing is similar to fishing in the aspect that one is using bait, yet different because the bait is not used to catch fish, but rather humans who fall for fake emails or websites created by cybercriminals to obtain personal information. These emails or websites often look too good to be true in the way that they offer a big prize if the user clicks on their hyperlink. The emails also pose a sense of urgency in the way that they may portray the email as an urgent matter in need of the user to perhaps ‘update’ their personal information in order to not face a consequence or suspension of their account.

What are the zones?

Just this year in January, the Puerto Rican government had accidentally allowed phishers to obtain 2.6 million dollars due to an email phishing scam. The email claimed that the government agency’s existing bank account could no longer be used, thus the money had to be transferred to a new bank account. The email detailed the new account to be used, which turned out to be fraudulent and under the cybercriminals control.
